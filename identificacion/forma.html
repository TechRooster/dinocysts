MORPHOLOGY


In general, the outlines of modern dinoflagellate cysts are relatively simple in comparison with fossil forms; that is, they are mainly spherical, subspherical, ovoidal, ellipsoidal or peridinioid. Also they can be rectangular, elongate, cruciform, pear-shaped, heart-shaped, besides, with pentagonal outline. Some brackish water cysts, however, vary in shape from simply spherical throught peridinioid to cruciform. It is notable that even in single species, environmental parameters such as salinity, temperature and nutrient can produce remarkable variation in the cyst shape. This is well investigated for Spiniferites cursiformis Wall et Dale and Tectatodinium pellitum Wall et Dale found in Quaternary sediments of the Black Sea (Wall et al., 1973). (Extracted from Matsuoka, 2000).



Matsuoka, K., & Fukuyo, Y. A. S. U. W. O. (2000). Technical guide for modern dinoflagellate cyst study. WESTPAC-HAB, Japan Society for the Promotion of Science, Tokyo, Japan, 47. pp. 22.
