VOTADINIUM SPINOSUM


Color: *Brown. **Brown.

Surface: **Wall thin. Smooth.

Shape: *Peridinioid cyst form. Cyst heart-shaped. **Dorsonventrally compressed with a rounded peridinioid outline and two antapical horns which are separated by a shallow antapical depression.

Central body: **49 to 54 um. (length).

Process length: **3 to 7 um. (length).

Process: *With spines/processes. Covered with numerous small spines. Spines randomly distributed. **Short, solid spines of equal length which are usually curved and irregularly distributed over the entire cyst.

Archeopyle: **Intercalary (2a), extends over the apex.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: -



DESCRIPTION:

Dorsoventrally compressed brown cyst with a rounded peridinioid outline and two antapical horns which are separated by a shallow antapical depression. Central body wall thin, with smooth surface, and covered by numerous short solid spines of equal length which are usually curved and are irregularly distributed over the entire cyst. The archeopyle, formed by loss of intercalary plate 2a, extends over the apex. (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al. 2020).



LOWEST STRATIGRAPHIC OCCURENCE:

Lower Pleistocene (Bujak and Matsuoka, 1986; Powell et al., 1990). (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).
