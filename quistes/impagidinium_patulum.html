IMPAGIDINIUM PATULUM


Color: *Transparent.

Surface: *Smooth. **Smooth to weakly finely granulate.

Shape: **Ovoidal.

Central body: **47 to 65 um. (diameter).

Process length: -

Process: -

Archeopyle: **Precingular. By loss of plate 3".

Paratabulation: *Complete. **Complete, except on the ventral side. **Well expressed by low sutural septa of uniform height over most of the cyst except in the ventral area.

Septa: *Equal length. Height of septa less than 1/3 of inner body. Smooth, low, robust. **Absent from the lower cingular margin where it meets the sulcus. Smooth distal margins.

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: **Surface, septa and tabulation.

Differs from:

Impagidinium sphaericum: **By the straight and smooth contact between the septa and cyst surface, and reduced tabulation in the ventral region.



DESCRIPTION:

Relatively large species with a weakly ovoidal central body and a smooth to weakly and finely granulate surface. The tabulation is well expressed by low sutural septa of uniform height over most of the cyst except in the ventral area. Septa are characteristically absent from the lower cingular margin where it meets the sulcus. Septa have smooth distal margins. Archeopyle formed by loss of precingular plate 3". (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).



LOWEST STRATIGRAPHIC OCURRENCE:

Although some occurrences of Impagidinium patulum have been reported from lowermost Paleogene strata (e.g., Head and Norris, 1989; Moshkovitz and Habib, 1993), its first common occurrence is more probably Lower Miocene (see discussion in Londeix, 2018).(Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).
