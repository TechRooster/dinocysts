SPINIFERITES BELERIUS


Color: *Transparent.

Surface: *Smooth to microreticulate wall. **Thin and smooth (scabrate to microgranular). Finely granular.

Shape: *Pear-shaped. With apical boss. Without apical protrusion. **Oval to round diamond-shaped, with an apical boss.

Central body: *More than 45 um. (length), **35 to 42 um. (length).

Process length: **7 to 10 um. (length). Posterior processes 10 to 15 um. (length).

Process: *Without distally connected processes. **Gonal. Trifurcarte. Frequently truncated. Variable in shape. Recurved bifurcate tips.

Archeopyle: -

Paratabulation: **High sutural membranaceus crests. Antapical flange is high membranous.

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: **High membranaceus flange. Clear apical node and crests from high antapical trumpet-shaped processes. ****Well-developed sutural membranes often observed between the processes, which are frequently truncated.

Differs from:

Spiniferites bentorii: **The latter is larger overall and has a hypocyst larger than the epicyst. ***Larger size, pear-shaped body and pronounced apical boss and the ocurrence of fenestrate bases in Spiniferites bentorii

Spiniferites bulloideus: ***in having an apical boss.

Spiniferites coniconcavus: **in having wider process bases and an apical boss.

Comparative table: Spiniferites



DESCRIPTION:

The small central body is oval, with a finely granular wall and an apical boss. The processes are gonal and connected by low crests with a typical process shape. This species was described as having a characteristic large ‘trumpet-shaped’ posterior process at the junction of plates 1 and 2 (Reid 1974, p. 597; but see Gurdebeke et al. 2018). (Extracted from Mertens et al., 2018).
