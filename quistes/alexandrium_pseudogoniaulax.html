ALEXANDRIUM PSEUDOGONIAULAX


Color: *Whitish to brownish-grey granules and a minor accumulation body.

Surface: -

Shape: *Spherical

Central body: *48 to 50 um.

Process length: -

Process: *No processes/spines.

Archeopyle: -

Paratabulation: *Some specimens possess a paratabulate wall.

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: *Size and sometimes paratabulation.



DESCRIPTION:

These cysts were spherical, 48–50 mm in diameter. Two types of cysts were observed depending on the wall and the cell content. One type possessed a paratabulate wall (Figs 11, 12), matching the description by Montresor et al. (1995), and the cell content was characterized by whitish to brownish-grey granules and a minor accumulation body (Fig. 12). Nevertheless, another cyst was observed apparently without a paratabulate wall when observed under the light microscope (Fig. 13). This type of cyst is probably a recently formed or a temporary cyst. Paratabulated and non-paratabulated cysts were observed in cultures at rather high concentrations (Figs 11, 15). SEM examination confirmed that there are cysts with very delicate paratabulation (Fig. 14), which suggests that at least some apparently nonparatabulated cysts in LM correspond to recently formed cysts in the process of paratabulation development (Extracted from Bravo et al., 2007).



ECOLOGY:

Two cysts of this species were detected in Alfacs Bay sediment. It is of interest that one paratabulate cyst was observed at a sediment depth of 4–5 cm and one apparently unparatabulate was detected in surface sediment (0–1 cm). This supports the suggestion above of time-dependent paratabulation. Both cysts successfully germinated and their respective cultures were identified as A. pseudogoniaulax (Fig. 24). In addition, cysts with unparatabulate walls, measuring 48–53 um. in diameter, were observed in sediment taken from Platera. Five of these cysts were isolated and only one successfully germinated to produce a culture of A. pseudogoniaulax (Extracted from Bravo et al., 2007).
