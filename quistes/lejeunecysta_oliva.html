LEJEUNECYSTA OLIVA


Color: *Brown.

Surface: *Smooth. Wall thin.

Shape: *Peridinioid. Apical and antapical protrusions have thickened wall. No striae present. **Two pointed solid tipped antapical horns.

Central body: -

Process length: -

Process: -

Archeopyle: **Intercalary elongated posterior/anteriorly.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: *Reflected. **Thickened ridges marking a narrow excavated circular cingulum.

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: **Small cingulum, thickened wall, apical and antapical horns.

Differs from:

Lejeunecysta paratenella: **in having smooth rather than denticular cingular ridges of L. paratenella. No spines, ridges or striae are present on the cyst wall.



DESCRIPTION:

Pen­ta­go­nal cysts in dor­so-ven­tral view with two poin­ted, so­lid tip­ped an­ta­pi­cal horns. Wall thin, sur­face smooth; thi­cke­ned ridges mar­king a nar­row ex­ca­va­ted, cir­cu­lar cin­gu­lum. Ar­che­o­pyle in­ter­cala­ry elonga­ted pos­te­ri­or/​an­te­rior­ly. (Extracted from MARUM).



DISTRIBUTION:

Lejeunecysta oliva is re­stric­ted to the co­as­tal en­vi­ron­ments of tem­pe­ra­te to sub­tro­pi­cal re­gi­ons in the eas­tern North and South At­lan­tic Oce­ans, the eas­tern North Pa­ci­fic Oce­an and the eas­tern South In­dian Oce­an. Ex­cep­ti­ons are for­med by a few re­cor­dings in the tro­pi­cal up­wel­ling re­gi­on off NW Af­ri­ca. It is pre­sent in up­wel­ling or ri­ver di­sch­ar­ge en­vi­ron­ments. Hig­hest ab­un­dan­ces (>2% and up to 9%) oc­cur in the vicini­ty of the ac­tive up­wel­ling cells off Se­ne­gal (NW Af­ri­ca) and South Af­ri­ca (SW Af­ri­ca). (Extracted from MARUM).
