LINGULODINIUM MACHAEROPHORUM


Color: *Transparent.

Surface: *Granulate. **Heavily granulate. **Microgranulate. Granulation is coarser on the conical base of the processes.

Shape: *Spherical. **Often broken.

Central body: **36 to 44 um. (diameter), ***31 to 54 um. (diameter).

Process length: ***2 to 21 um. (length).

Process: *Hollow to fibrous. Distal tips closed. Can have spinulae. **Circular base and terminate as pointed tip. Smooth surface, small spinules towards the top of processes. Much variability in length and morphology. ***Smooth surface, some granules in tips of processes. Bases can vary from short to long from rounded to acuminate tips.

Archeopyle: *Precingular, loss of one to five plates. **Never observed, not important for identification. ***Epicystal and variable morphology.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: ***Processes. Wider basis and round closed tops. Distal parts covered by spinules.

Differs from:

Operculodinium israelianum: ***in its archeopyle, also O. israelianum has absence of striae.

Comparative table: Transparent/Hollow processes



DESCRIPTION:

Spherical central body with finely granulate surface. Processes are nontabular and hollow, arising from a circular base and tapering distally. Process length can vary from a few micrometers to >30 μm, but is usually fairly uniform for each individual. Processes usually have a smooth surface over most of their length but small spinules often occur over the distal part. The distal ends of the processes are closed and tend towards flattening, and usually terminate as a pointed or blunt tip, but distinctly capitate terminations may occur especially when processes are short. Processes may show slight constriction at their bases. The range of process variability is illustrated by Kokinos and Anderson (1995) and Mertens et al. (2009). Archeopyle formed by loss of between one and six precingular plates, but with the apical plates remaining attached to the sulcus even when all six precingular plates are lost. The margin of the archeopyle has rounded angles. (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).



STRATIGRAPHIC OCCURRENCE:

Upper Paleocene (Edwards et al., 1984; Heilmann-Clausen, 1985). (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).
