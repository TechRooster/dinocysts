ECHINIDINIUM TRANSPARANTUM


Color: *Brown.

Surface: **Smooth

Shape: *Spherical

Central body: **22 to 36 um.

Process length: **5 to 14 um.

Process: *Solid. Acuminate tips. Base circular to irregularly rectangular.

Archeopyle: **Theropylic. Extending at least half of the cyst body.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: ***Smaller size of central body and relatively longer processes

Comparative table: RSBC

Differs from:

Echinidinium zonneveldiae: ***in its smaller size and relatively longer processes.

Nia acanthocysta: ***in its smaller size and relatively longer processes.



DESCRIPTION:

Brown cyst with spherical central body that has a smooth to faintly granulate surface bearing solid, acuminate, rigid, and transparent processes. Process bases (sub-)circular to irregularly rectangular. Archeopyle theropylic. (Extracted from Radi et al., 2013).



ECOLOGY:

Light to medium brown spheroidal central body, its surface smooth with occasional scattered granules or spinules. Central body wall thickness is b0.3 μm. Processes are solid with smooth shafts, taper to ﬁne points, and have circular to irregular rectangular bases. Process width is between about 1.3 and 3.0 μm. Wider processes always expanded at the base. Theropylic archeopyle, presumably cingular with a straight split that may extend at least half way around the cyst. (Extracted from Radi et al., 2013).
