VOTADINIUM CALVUM


Color: *Brown. **Dark brown.

Surface: **Smooth to microgranular. Wall unthickened at the horns.

Shape: *Peridinioid cyst form. Cyst heart-shaped, rounded anpical and antapical horns. No striae or granulation. **Dorsoventrally compressed cyst with a strongly rounded peridinioid outline. Apical horn strongly reduced. The rounded, equally-developed antapical horns are separated by a shallow antapical depression.

Central body: *54 to 72 um. (length).

Process length: -

Process: -

Archeopyle: **Intercalary (2a), extends over the apex.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: -



DESCRIPTION:

Dorsoventrally compressed cyst with a strongly rounded peridinioid outline. The wall is thin, dark brown, and has a smooth to microgranular surface. The apical horn is strongly reduced, represented by a light swelling such that the 2a intercalary archeopyle extends over the apex. The rounded, equally-developed antapical horns are separated by a shallow antapical depression. The wall is unthickened at the horns. (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).



STRATIGRAPHIC RANGE:

Upper Pliocene (Mudie, 1987). Isolated observations from the Lower Miocene by Londeix and Jan du Chêne (1998, not illustrated) appear to conform with the morphology of Quaternary specimens (unpublished observations by LL). (Extracted from Van Nieuwenhove et al., 2020).
