ARCHAEPERIDINIUM SAANICHI


Color: *Brown. **Dark brown when cell content is present and hazel brown when empty. Living cysts have abundant orange granules.

Surface: *Smooth to finely granulate. Wall thin. **Smooth. Wall thin.

Shape: *Spherical to subspherical.

Central body: * > 37 um. **36 to 52 um. (diameter).

Process length: **2.8 to 7.8 um. (length).

Process: *Hollow. Aculeate. Base circular. **Numerous equidistant, non-tabular processes. Bumps or reduced processes may be observed between processes. Tapering, hollow and generally erect but can be slightly inflected, and always end in minute distal expansion. Never fused or branched at their ends. Circular base.

Archeopyle: *Large. **Theropylic, intercalary, corresponds to the large 2a plate.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: -



DESCRIPTION:

Cysts have a spherical to subspherical central body, and are dark brown when cell content is present and hazel brown when empty. The living cysts have abundant orange granules. The central body wall is thin with a smooth surface densely packed with numerous equidistant, non-tabular processes (15–22 processes per 10 × 10 μm). Bumps or reduced processes may be observed between processes. Processes are tapering, hollow and generally erect but can be slightly inflected, and always end in minute distal expansions. The processes are never fused or branched at their ends. The process bases are circular in cross-section. The archeopyle is intercalary and theropylic and corresponds to the large 2a plate. Based on Mertens et al. (2012b). (Extracted from Mertens et al., 2019).



ECOLOGY:

Upper Quaternary to modern sediments from the Santa Barbara Basin and California margin (Bringué et al., 2014; Pospelova et al., 2015) and modern sediments from Vancouver Island (Mertens et al., 2012b). (Extracted from Mertens et al., 2019).
