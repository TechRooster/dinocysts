PYXIDINOPSIS BRABOI


Color: -

Surface: *Rigid with characteristic high (1.5 to 2 um.) irregular, complete or incomplete microreticulation over surface. Lumina with small (0.3-0.5 um.) and large (0.5 -4 um.) diameters on the same cysts. Muri are relatively thick (0.3-0.5 um.) and of event height over all surface. Smooth lumina and muri. Thickness: 1.5 to 2 um.

Shape: *Spherical. Small apical protuberance, involving both pedium and luxuria.

Central body: *40 um. diameter including luxuria.

Process length: -

Process: -

Archeopyle: *Precingular (1P), isdodeltacamerate, formed by loss of plate 3", ornamentation extends to archeopyle borders. Free operculum.

Paratabulation: *Not reflected.

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: *Reticulation, size, thickness of wall, archeopyle shape.

Differs from:

Pyxidinopsis delicata: *Differs from P. braboi in having a coarser reticulation with crenulate muri, larger overall size (51 to 62 um.) and thicker wall (2 to 4 um.).

Pyxidinopsis waipawaensis: *Differs from P. braboi in its coarser microreticulation and having a rounded pentagonal to quadrangular archeopyle bordered by a low ridge.

Pyxidinopsis reticulata: *Differs from P. braboi due to its ornamentation within the reticulum.



DESCRIPTION:

Small, spheroidal, proximate cysts; wall rigid with characteristic high (ca. 1.5–2.0 mm), irregular, complete or incomplete microreticulation over entire cyst surface. Lumina with small (ca. 0.3–0.5 mm) and large (ca. 0.5–4 mm) diameters occur on same cyst. Larger lumina dominate. Muri are relatively thick (0.3–0.5 mm) and of even height over entire cyst. Surface of muri and lumina smooth. Tabulation not reﬂected in lumen distribution. Homogeneous and smooth pedium up to about 0.3 mm thick, usually less. Archeopyle precingular (1P), iso-deltacamerate, formed by loss of plate 30; ornamentation extends to archeopyle borders. Operculum free. Archeopyle sides 2–4 (sensu de Verteuil and Norris, 1996, text-ﬁg. 21, p. 100) are slightly concave. Archeopyle sides 1 or 5 are reentrant near their midpoint (Fig. 6.3, 6.8). Angles 2` 3 and 3` 4 slightly rounded. No accessory sutures. Small apical protuberance (ca. 1.0 mm), involving both pedium and luxuria, observed on single specimen; not recognized on other specimens possibly due to cyst orientation. No other indications of tabulation. Cysts are susceptible to uptake of safranin-O stain. (Extracted from DeSchepper, 2004).



OCCURRENCE:

Basal Shelly Unit, Oorderen Sands and Kruisschans Sands members of the Lillo Formation at the Deurganck Dock; Basal Shelly Unit and Oorderen Sands Member at the Verrebroek Dock. Upper Lower and/or lower Upper Pliocene. (Extracted from DeSchepper, 2004).
