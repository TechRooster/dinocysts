SPINIFERITES MULTISPHAERUS


Color: *Transparent.

Surface: *Cyst wall with bubble-like elements. **Wall is relatively thick and contains a single layer of vacuoles.

Shape: *Pear-shaped. With apical boss. Without apical protrusion. **Ovoid to pear-shaped with a pronounced apical protuberance.

Central body: **42.6 to 66.5 um. (diameter).

Process length: **1.5 to 8 um. (length).

Process: *Without distally connected processes. Processes trifurcate with bubble-like elements. **Processes are stubby, furcate and also contain vacuoles.

Archeopyle: **The archeopyle is formed by the loss of plate 3" and is reduced.

Paratabulation: **Tabulation is clearly expressed and is typical of the genus.

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: **Presence of vacuoles in the cyst wall, processes and sutural septa.

Differs from:

Spiniferites bentorii: **by the presence of vacuoles and "reticulate" surface. S bentorii surface appears smooth to microgranular.

Spiniferites ludhamensis: **the latter has a wall structure comparable to bubble-wrap.



DESCRIPTION:

Ovoid to pear-shaped central body with a pronounced apical protuberance. Wall is relatively thick and contains a single layer of vacuoles. Processes are stubby, furcate and also contain vacuoles. Tabulation is clearly expressed and is typical of the genus. The archeopyle is formed by loss of plate 3”. (Based on Price & Pospelova 2014, p.7–13.) (Extracted from Mertens et al., 2018).
