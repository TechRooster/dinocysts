ECHINIDINIUM DELICATUM


Color: *Brown.

Surface: *Smooth.

Shape: *Spherical.

Central body: **17 to 25 um.

Process length: **2 to 4 um.

Process: *Hollow. Acuminate tips. Base circular. **Rigid and distally closed.

Archeopyle: **Theropylic.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: -

Cingulum: -

Sulcus: -

Distiguishing characteristics: **Small central body.

Comparative table: RSBC



DESCRIPTION:

Pale brown cyst with smooth spheroidal central body bearing more or less regularly distributed processes. Processes are rigid, hollow, distally closed and acuminate, and have sub-circular bases. Archeopyle theropylic. (Extracted from Radi et al. 2013).



ECOLOGY:

Described initially from the Arabian Sea (Zonneveld, 1997), this species has since been found in coastal and estuarine environments of the northeastern Pacific (Radi et al., 2007; Pospelova et al., 2008, 2010; Vásquez-Bedoya et al., 2008; Krepakevich and Pospelova, 2010; Limoges et al., 2010; Price and Pospelova, 2011). In the modern Northern Hemisphere database, E. delicatum is found in the same environments as E. granulatum. (Extracted from Radi et al. 2013).
