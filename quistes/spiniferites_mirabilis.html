SPINIFERITES MIRABILIS


Color: *Transparent.

Surface: *Smooth cyst wall **Smooth to granular.

Shape: *Spherical. With antapical protrusion **Ovoid.

Central body: **40 to 70 um. (length), 35 to 60 um. (width).

Process length: *Processes on triple junctions. Bifurcate and trifurcate. Antapical processes connected forming a crown-shape. **Gonal as well as intergonal, slender and connected by generally low to barely discernible crests. Prominent antapical flange connects the tow antapical processes.

Process: **15 to 22 um. (length).

Archeopyle: **Formed by loss of plate 3", and is reduced.

Paratabulation: -

Septa: *Can have septa along sutures.

Cingulum: **Displaced by two times its width.

Sulcus: **Slightly oblique.

Distiguishing characteristics: **Antapical flange and the presence of intergonal processes.

Differs from:

: **in having an antapical flange.

: **by the presence of intergonal processes.



DESCRIPTION:

Central body ovoid with a smooth to granular surface. The processes are gonal as well as intergonal, slender, and connected by generally low to barely discernible crests. However, a prominent antapical flange connects the two antapical processes. The sulcus is slightly oblique and the cingulum is displaced by two times its width. The archeopyle is formed by the loss of plate 3” and is reduced. (Based on Rossignol 1964, p.86–87, and new observations by AL, LL and KNM.) (Extracted from Mertens et al., 2018).
